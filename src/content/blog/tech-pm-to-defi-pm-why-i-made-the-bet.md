# Why I'm Betting 60 Days on DeFi

I've spent the last decade building software products. Financial platforms, insurance systems, a compliance tool used by 50,000 regulated users, and — for about eight months — a live crypto exchange.

That last one changed something.

Working on Stellarity, a white-label DEX, I got a close look at what it actually takes to run a production DeFi product: the ops complexity, the user trust problems, the gap between what protocols promise and what they deliver. I wasn't a tourist. I was in the engine room.

Then I stepped back into traditional software. And I couldn't stop thinking about that gap.

---

## The role that made me move

A few weeks ago I came across a job posting from Orca: *Web3 Product Manager, On-Chain Private Markets*.

I read it three times.

The role sits at the intersection of two things I've spent years doing: building regulated financial systems (settlement precision, audit trails, compliance stakeholders as daily partners) and working inside live DeFi infrastructure. Orca explicitly lists TradFi background as *strongly preferred*. Most DeFi PM candidates don't have it.

I do. But I have a gap.

I know how exchanges work. I don't yet know on-chain private markets — the tokenization of real-world assets, private credit protocols, the specific technical limitations that make this space hard. That's what the next 60 days are for.

---

## What I'm doing

I've mapped out a 60-day plan:

- **Weeks 1–2:** Deep dive on RWA protocols — Centrifuge, Maple Finance, Ondo Finance, the mechanics of on-chain private credit
- **Weeks 3–4:** Build technical depth — reading contract state, understanding protocol limitations well enough to discuss them with engineers
- **Weeks 5–6:** Build something real — an RWA portfolio dashboard that demonstrates I understand the product space, not just the theory
- **Weeks 7–8:** Apply — Orca first, then Centrifuge, Maple Finance, Ondo Finance, and a shortlist of similar companies

I'm documenting it here, post by post, as it happens. Not a polished retrospective — the actual journey, including what I get wrong.

## Why share it publicly?

Three reasons.

First, accountability. A public commitment is harder to abandon than a private one.

Second, there's almost nothing written for Tech PMs making this transition. Plenty of developer tutorials. Almost nothing for someone coming from a product background who already understands financial systems and wants to understand DeFi at a protocol level.

Third, the best way to get a job at a protocol is to demonstrate you understand the space before you apply. This series is that demonstration.

## FAQ

**What does a Tech PM to DeFi PM transition look like?**
It means taking existing skills in product management, financial systems, and software delivery and applying them to decentralized finance protocols. The gap is domain knowledge — understanding RWA mechanics, protocol limitations, and on-chain data — not seniority or PM craft.

**What is Orca in DeFi?**
Orca is a Web3 protocol focused on on-chain private markets — bringing private credit, private equity, and structured finance instruments onto the blockchain with composability, transparent NAV, and programmable yield distribution.

**Do you need to know Solidity to be a DeFi PM?**
No. DeFi protocol PM roles require understanding *what contracts do* — reading state, interpreting events, discussing limitations with engineers — not writing Solidity. The technical bar is closer to ethers.js and viem than smart contract development.

**What is RWA in crypto?**
RWA stands for Real World Assets — the tokenization of real-world financial instruments (treasuries, bonds, private credit, real estate) on-chain. Protocols like Ondo Finance (tokenized US treasuries), Centrifuge (on-chain private credit), and Maple Finance (institutional lending) are the leading examples in 2026.

**Why is TradFi background valuable for DeFi PM roles?**
On-chain private markets are replicating TradFi private credit and structured finance infrastructure on-chain. PMs who understand settlement precision, audit trails, compliance stakeholders, and credit risk assessment from TradFi can immediately contribute where most crypto-native PMs have a gap.
